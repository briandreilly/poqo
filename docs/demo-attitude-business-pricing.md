# poqo Demo: Attitude Example — Startup Pricing

This example shows the same reasoning decision expressed through different response attitudes.

---

## Input

A startup should always lower prices if it wants to grow faster.

---

## normal

“Always” makes this too broad. Growth also depends on margins, customer value, and competition.

---

## challenge

Why “always”? Lower prices may increase acquisition, but they can also damage margins and sustainability. What conditions are you assuming?

---

## difficult

That claim is too absolute to accept. Price cuts are not automatically growth-positive. State the actual conditions or drop “always.”

---

## What stays the same

All three responses reject the claim for being overgeneralized and insufficiently bounded.

---

## What changes

Only the response attitude changes:
- `normal` = clarifies
- `challenge` = presses on assumptions
- `difficult` = rejects weak framing faster
